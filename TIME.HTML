TIMELINE

HERE WE PRESENT YOU A BRIEF TIMELINE OF INDIA'S CRICKET HISTORY

  • 1792The Calcutta Cricket Club already in existence (perhaps the second oldest cricket club in the world) as a match was played against Barrackpore and Dum Dum.
  • 1854First known publication on cricket in India -- Calcutta Cricket Club matches 1844-54 -- this is also the first known book of scores outside the British Isles.
  • 1867First known instructional book on cricket in Hindi: the same book translated into Urdu in 1868: both were published in Agra, United Provinces
  • 1884 First match between Bombay Gymkhana (Europeans) and Parsi Gymkhana: latter won by innings and 38 runs. From this encounter developed the Bombay Presidency matches and later Tournaments
  • 1890 Parsi Cricket Club formed in Shanghai: probably the first Indian cricket club to be established outside India.
  • 1902 Third tour by English team to India: Oxford University Authentics, who established a new Indian record by scoring 696 v. Peshawar
  • 1908 First Presidency match in Madras -- Europeans v. Indians. The match lapsed until 1915 when it was revived, and played regularly until 1946.
  • 1922First All India Cricket tournament at New Delhi, won by Maharajah of Patiala's XI. The tournament was played in most years until the early 1930s
  • 1931 In April the first Indian cricket magazine commenced -- Illustrated Cricket & Sporting News: name changed in 1932 to Indian Cricketer but did not last beyond 1933.
  • 1934First issue of a fresh Indian cricket magazine, in October: Indian Cricket, a well produced publication, which however ceased shortly after the outbreak of war in 1939.
  • 1935-36Rohinton Baria Tournament instituted for inter-University cricket: not first-class. The Marajah of Patiala sponsored the first (unofficial) tour by Australians to India: won two and drew two of the unofficial Tests
  • 1937Bombay Tournament became Pentangular with the admission of The Rest. This tournament lasted until 1945-6 in communal form, and for two more seasons thereafter as a non-communal tournament.
  • 1940Maharashtra made 798 v. Northern India at Poona, establishing a fresh Indian record
  • 1950First Commonwealth tour to India: won two, lost one and drew two of the unofficial tests
  • 1953 First tour to West Indies by India: lost one and drew four Tests.
  • 1956First tour to India by official Australian team (third in all): won two and drew one Test.
  • 1962Moin-ud-Dowlah Gold Cup Tournament successfully revived.
  • 1966Third tour by West Indies to India.
